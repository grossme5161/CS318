Jigglypuff

Jigglypuff is a playable character, but it must be unlocked by clearing the 1-Player mode with any character. Jigglypuff has one of the best recoveries in the game and can use its low-power aerial attacks to combo larger and heavier opponents easily, but it also the lightest character and thus very easy to knock off the stage. Jigglypuff shares the Saffron City stage with Pikachu.

Pikachu

Pikachu is a playable character, and the only Pokémon that is playable from the start of the game. Its main stage is Saffron City. In the hands of a skilled player, Pikachu is not to be taken lightly. Alongside electrical attacks, it has very good aerial attacks and superb, but hard to master, recovery move. Pikachu is also one of the lightest characters in the game, making it easy for its foes to launch it. Despite this, top-level SSB players have placed Pikachu at the top of their list, theoretically making it the best character in the game.

Mewtwo

Mewtwo (Japanese: ミュウツー Mewtwo) is a playable character in the Super Smash Bros. series. It was playable in Super Smash Bros. Melee for the Nintendo GameCube, was absent from Super Smash Bros. Brawl, but returned as downloadable content for Super Smash Bros. for Nintendo 3DS/Wii U. In Melee, its voice and characterization are based on the Japanese portrayal of the original Mewtwo from the anime, although its spoken lines are removed in non-Japanese versions rather than being dubbed.

Peeko

Peeko is kidnapped by a Team Magma Grunt in Pokémon Ruby and Omega Ruby, or a Team Aqua Grunt grunt in Pokémon Sapphire, Emerald, and Alpha Sapphire. In either case, the player must go to Rusturf Tunnel and defeat the grunt to rescue her. After she is rescued and returned to Mr. Briney, he will return to his cottage and later offer to sail the player to Dewford Town and Slateport City.

Charizard

Charizard (Japanese: リザードン Lizardon) is a playable character in the Super Smash Bros. series. He originally appeared in the series when being summoned from a Poké Ball, and later became a playable character in Brawl, first as a part of the Pokémon Trainer's party, and later as an independent character in subsequent installments.

Uxie

Uxie (Japanese: ユクシー Yuxie) is a non-player character in Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Time and Explorers of Darkness and Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Explorers of Sky. He is responsible for guarding the Time Gear in Fogbound Lake. Despite being genderless in the core series, both Uxie and Azelf are referred to as males, while Mesprit is referred to as a female; however, during gameplay they are treated as genderless.
